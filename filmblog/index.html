LEARN FILM

Documenting my experience with disposable cameras.

How I started

Two years ago I bought my first disposable film camera- a bright yellow KODAK Power Flash 35mm Single Use. I was intrigued by the idea of having a limited number of shots. I thought it would encourage me to be more spontaneous and help me capture memorable experiences in a tangibel way.

Tutorial

The hardest part for me was taking the first picture using my camera. I wanted everything about it to be perfect- the lighting, framing, subject, etc. However, the best way to get started IS to take pictures.

Getting a Disposable Camera

You can easily pick up a disposable camera from Walmart or Target for around $16. I find that there are usually always a couple of them in the Electronics section. Sometimes there are even multi packs that offer them at better value!

Taking a Picture

Taking pictures are really simple. Unlike digital cameras that allow you to control photo settings, disposable cameras are meant to be "point and shoot". Once you have locked in on a specific shot you are interested in, line up the subject by peering through the viewfinder. As a rule of hand, you want to ensure that the subject is at least 3 feet away so that it is in focus. From my experience, you should also use your flash freely. When the lighting is a bit dark or when the backdrop is brighter, turning on the flash really helps capture the image how you would want it to look.

Getting Your Physical and Digital Images

The cameras themselves have instructions on how to get your photos developed. KODAK cameras have partnerships with CVS, while FujiFilm cameras are partnered with Walmart Camera. In any case, getting your pictures are really simple and just involve you dropping off your camera at the specified store and wait for 3-4 weeks to pick up your photos.
